FAQ


When is the application deadline?

Our application deadline is 11:59pm PT on Wednesday, December 31, 2014.


What is a “fellow” anyway?

When we say “fellow” we don’t mean “man.” A fellow is someone who “shares a particular activity, quality, or condition with someone.” Fellowship programs bring together a diverse collection of people with shared skills and experiences to spend time and collaborate together.

I am not an engineer. Should I still apply?

Absolutely! There is no requirement for a particular programming language or development skillset. A lot of policy change happens through community organizing, campaign design and other non-engineering skill sets.

What type of applicants are you looking for?

If you have a knack for coding and an interest in contributing your skills to internet policy projects, you are the type of person we are looking for. There is no “typical” Fellow.

We are an inclusive program and seek Fellows that reflect the diversity of the communities we serve. Women and other under-represented participants in technology are strongly encouraged to apply for a Ford-Mozilla Open Web Fellowship.

I don’t speak English very well or live in the U.S. Can I still participate?

We are an international program and welcome participants from around the world. You do not need to be a U.S. citizen to participate in the program. We assist Fellows with any necessary visa applications for working and traveling during the Fellowship. Perfect English is not a requirement, however please be aware that it may be challenging to participate in some aspects of our program if your English is limited.

Where will the fellows be based? How are the fellows matched with organizations?

Fellows will primarily be based in their host organizations. They will also spend time working as a cohort in the Mozilla San Francisco office.

In 2015, the Fellows will be based with the ACLU, Amnesty International, Free Press, Open Technology Institute, and Public Knowledge.

We make every attempt to match skills of Fellows with needs of partner organizations when matching Fellow to organization.

What are you looking for with the example projects on the application?

We want to see three projects that you are proud of. Tell us why you’re excited about the project, and if you made the project as part of a team, describe the contribution that you made. The examples you include are not shared beyond the small pool of application reviewers and host organizations.

What if my example projects don’t include open source or technology contributions?

That’s ok. We understand that a lot of people have done work that is not open source and that sometimes policy and advocacy work is not technology specific. You will be building in the open and contributing back to open source projects as a Fellow, but it is not a requirement to have done this before applying.

What is the timeline after the Fellowship deadline closes?

The Fellowship application opened on September 30, 2014 and closes December 31, 2014. We’ll be reviewing applications in the beginning of 2015 and making a decision on the final fellows for the 2015 class. We expect the fellowship to begin in May 2015.

What kind of competition are you expecting with the Fellowship?

This is the first year of the Ford-Mozilla Open Web Fellows search process, so it’s difficult to gauge the level of competition. For comparison, the Mozilla-Knight OpenNews Fellowship program receives hundreds of qualified applications for a limited amount of Fellowship openings.
In terms of the applicant pool, we are committed to matching the skills of the Fellow to the needs of the host organization. It’s a very individualized matching process.

How does the application review and matching process work?

  • Applications are submitted online and are visible at the time they are submitted, but formal review does not begin until after the application deadline closes on December 31.
  • Mozilla’s policy and advocacy staff review all of the applications and cull the batch to a manageable number for the partner organizations to review.
  • The partners review this shorter list and submit a list of the five people they are most interested in learning more about.
  • These semifinalists are contacted for interviews. Applicants who are not moving forward in the selection process are notified.
  • Mozilla’s policy and advocacy team conduct short, standardized interviews with the semifinalists, asking a few questions that are about vision and skills/practical considerations.
  • The notes from these interviews are submitted back to the host organizations for review, and the hosts are able to pick their top three finalists from the entire pool of semifinalists, not just the five they initially submitted.
  • The semifinalists that are not moving forward are notified of their application status.
  • The host organization and Mozilla’s policy and advocacy team has a much longer interview with each of the partner organization’s top three candidates. This interview is a chance for the host organization and the finalist to learn more about each other and what to expect from a Fellowship at that host organization.
  • In consultation with Mozilla’s policy and advocacy team and the partner organizations, the final Fellow selection is made for each organization.
  • The selected Fellows are notified and given an opportunity to ask additional questions of the partner organization and Mozilla.
  • The finalists that were not selected as Fellows are notified of their application status.

What is the timeline for the Fellowship?

2015 Fellowships begin in May 2015 and last for 10 months. Each Fellows’ start date will be individualized for the needs of that Fellow and the partner organization.

What type of projects do the fellows work on?

That is dictated by the host organization and the Fellow. We expect that these projects will reflect a range of policy and advocacy activities, including program/campaign development, technology issue analysis, data crunching, and more. We’re looking for talented people who want to collaborate with host organizations to advance existing projects and to create new ones. In collaboration with Mozilla, the other Fellows, and the host organizations, Fellows get a lot of room to figure out what the projects will be.

Are you still accepting applications for host organizations?

No, we are not accepting host applications at this time. But there are many ways to collaborate with Mozilla’s policy & advocacy efforts. If you are interested in exploring collaboration, email us at advocacy@mozilla.com.

How do I contact you?

We’re very reachable. You can e-mail us at advocacy@mozilla.com or find us on Twitter at @MozillaAdvocacy. You can also join our mailing list and monthly community calls at https://wiki.mozilla.org/Netpolicy. We’d love to hear from you and are happy to answer any questions you may have.
